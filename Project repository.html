3D printing:

I have 3d printed gears, scaled down model, self-designed 3d models, joints, and many more. Prints originate from Thingivers, self-designed, and other online platforms where STL files are uploaded. It was important to ensure the first layer adhered well to the build plate as it is the foundation of the whole print. I have five 3D printers at home, which are used frequently for my business, 3D Kinetic which produces 3D printed custom name stands/tags and a wide variety of unique cookie cutters. Below are suggested settings for PLA and TPU.

Material: PLA
Nozel temp: 200-220℃
Print bed temp: 60℃
print speed: 50-80mm/s
Material: TPU
Nozel temp: 200-230℃
Print bed temp: 60℃
print speed:10-20mm/s
Speed varies with setup (direct drive/bowden extrusion). Adhesion is not critical in most prints, however prints which have little surface contact to print bed should use raft for better base adhesion. Raft can also be used when printing gears as they help prevent elephant's foot.

Shirt Printing:

I have customised my own shirt, using Canva and inkscape as my sketch platform and saved the file as png into a thumbdrive The method of printing used was dye sublimation, where the ink from the print is dyed on to each individual treade This is how heat sublimation printing work:
Set your heat press to 400 degrees.
Time: 45-60 seconds. Pressure: Medium. Place sheets of white copy paper or parchment paper inside your shirt to prevent bleed through. Position your transfer face down (use heat tape if desired) and cover with Teflon sheet.
Press.
Remove transfer immediately.
